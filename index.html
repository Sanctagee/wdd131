Ebonyi State, Nigeria

Ebonyi State, nicknamed "The Salt of the Nation", is a hidden gem in Nigeria's South-East geopolitical zone. Created in 1996 from parts of Enugu and Abia States. It has about 13 Local Government Areas, and it's renowned for:

  • Mineral wealth: Home to massive salt deposits at the Okposi and Uburu salt lakes, limestone, and lead/zinc ores
  • Agricultural prowess: Nigeria's largest producer of rice and cassava, with the famous Abakaliki rice
  • Natural wonders: Ndibe beach (one of Africa's deepest), Abakaliki Green Lake, and the scenic Oferekpe Waterfront
  • Cultural heritage: The iconic Ojiji festival celebrating new yams and the traditional Okpregede music

The state is emerging as an education hub with high institutions such as:

  • Ebonyi State University (EBSU)
  • Federal College of Agriculture (Ishiagu)
  • Alex Ekwueme Federal University (AE-FUNAI)
